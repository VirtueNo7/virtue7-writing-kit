# 5. Projection Contracts

A projection contract defines how a canonical content unit becomes a particular format. It states what must remain, what may transform, and how success will be tested.

| Format | Must preserve | May transform | Primary validation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kernel | meaning, mechanism, boundaries | wording | source is inspectable and governing |
| Matrix | unit identity, relations, coverage | order, columns, density | whole system is accurate at a glance |
| Plan | responsibilities, sequence, evidence needs | grouping, chapter count | every promise has a structural home |
| Handbook | definitions, mechanisms, examples, cautions | modular sequence and density | units work independently and together |
| Professional chapter | thesis, evidence integrity, reader value | scene order, pacing, voice | narrative deepens rather than replaces knowledge |
| Full book | kernel, unit relations, whole-book promise | rhythm, escalation, transitions | sustained coherence and earned conclusion |
| Course | learning outcomes and safe practice | activities, timing, assessment | learner can demonstrate understanding |
| Audio or video | central question, mechanism, conclusion | performance and scene order | meaning survives time-based delivery |
| Software or cards | canonical labels and bounded prompts | interaction and feedback | interface does not misrepresent or diagnose |

A projection contract prevents adaptation from becoming deadline improvisation, the ancient rite by which carefully governed meaning is sacrificed to “something punchier.”
