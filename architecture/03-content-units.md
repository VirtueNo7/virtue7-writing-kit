# 3. Canonical Content Units

A content unit is the smallest independently governed block that can expand into a complete reading, learning, or media experience.

Depending on the book, a unit may be:

- one life period;
- one transformation;
- one law or principle;
- one case;
- one method;
- one problem and solution;
- one question and discovery;
- one cause and consequence;
- one chapter responsibility.

## Required unit fields

| Field | Question | Minimum condition |
|---|---|---|
| Identity | What is this unit? | Stable ID, title, role, version, state |
| Central tension | What governs it? | Expressible in ordinary language |
| Definitions | What do key terms mean? | Clear, bounded, distinguishable |
| Mechanism | How does it produce outcomes? | Sequence or causation is explicit |
| Evidence | What supports it? | Sources and uncertainty are recorded |
| Narrative role | Why is this story or example present? | It demonstrates a named responsibility |
| Misconceptions | What might be confused? | At least one useful distinction |
| Application | How does it reach the reader? | Reader value is visible |
| Compression | What must remain? | Paragraph, sentence, and line forms |
| Governance | Who approved it? | Validation and approval state visible |

## Modularity rule

A unit may be drafted, tested, replaced, or reused without forcing the entire book to be rewritten. Modularity is not permission to assemble a book like flat-pack furniture. Whole-book rhythm, escalation, contrast, and emotional coherence still require developmental editing.
