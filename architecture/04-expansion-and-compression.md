# 4. Expansion and Compression

## Expansion

Expansion increases resolution by adding one or more authorised functions. It does not merely increase word count.

Every expansion must identify what it adds:

- **Clarify:** define terms and distinctions.
- **Explain:** reveal mechanism, sequence, or causation.
- **Demonstrate:** use evidence, examples, scenes, or documented stories.
- **Compare:** reveal alternatives, contrasts, and trade-offs.
- **Apply:** connect the unit to the reader, profession, context, or decision.
- **Practise:** introduce a proportionate action, reflection, or exercise.
- **Adapt:** translate the source for another audience, medium, locale, or accessibility need.

Before expanding, declare:

- source artifact and resolution;
- target artifact and resolution;
- canonical dependencies;
- named expansion functions;
- evidence requirements;
- freedoms allowed by the target format;
- invariants that may not change.

## Compression

Compression removes delivery-specific detail while preserving the approved meaning and boundaries.

Every approved unit should maintain at least four compressed forms:

1. **Full unit summary:** definition, mechanism, evidence role, contrast, and practical implication in one paragraph.
2. **Operational sentence:** the pattern, decision, or causal sequence in one or two sentences.
3. **Decision line or governing question:** the human choice in ordinary language.
4. **Kernel formula:** the irreducible relation.

Compression is invalid when it:

- reverses direction;
- removes a decisive qualification;
- converts a possibility into certainty;
- turns a pattern into an identity label;
- makes a stronger factual claim than the evidence supports;
- preserves keywords while changing the actual meaning.
