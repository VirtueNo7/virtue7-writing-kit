# Workflow 4 - Collapse

## Purpose

Reduce a book, chapter, or content unit to a lower resolution while preserving its governing meaning, direction, mechanism, qualifications, and boundaries.

## Procedure

1. Identify the approved source artifact and target resolution.
2. Extract claims, definitions, causal or logical relations, evidence status, reader value, and boundaries.
3. Remove examples, scenes, transitions, performance, and delivery-specific detail that are not required at the target resolution.
4. Preserve decisive qualifications, uncertainty, counterexamples, and prohibited interpretations.
5. Produce the requested compressed form.
6. Compare it to the canonical kernel and invariant set.
7. Report any information that could not be removed safely.

## Standard collapse outputs

- `R6 -> R4`: full book to handbook.
- `R6 -> R3`: full book to plan.
- `R6 -> R2`: full book to matrix.
- `R5 -> R0`: chapter to kernel lineage.
- `Unit -> paragraph -> sentence -> question -> formula`.

## Failure condition

Do not call a summary successful merely because it is shorter. A compressed output fails when a reasonable reader would recover a different idea from it.
