# Workflow 5 - Inspect and Validate

## Validation layers

### 1. Structural

- Is there one approved kernel?
- Does every reader promise have a structural home?
- Are units distinct, ordered, and proportionate?
- Was a representative unit validated before scaling?

### 2. Semantic

- Do definitions remain consistent?
- Does each unit preserve the governing relation?
- Do compressed forms recover the kernel?
- Has any boundary or qualification disappeared?

### 3. Evidence

- Are factual claims mapped to sources?
- Are source quality, uncertainty, rights, and disputes visible?
- Are quotations exact and verified?
- Has interpretation been disguised as fact?

### 4. Narrative

- Does every scene perform a structural function?
- Are people represented with complexity and fairness?
- Does the narrative deepen the book rather than replace it?
- Are transitions, escalation, pacing, and conclusion earned?

### 5. Reader value

- Can the intended reader understand the language?
- Does the book deliver its stated change or understanding?
- Are practical elements proportionate and safe?
- Does each chapter leave something useful and memorable?

### 6. Originality

- Is the voice appropriate without imitating a named author?
- Are metaphors and structures original to this project?
- Has generic AI filler been removed?

### 7. White-label boundary

- No Virtue7 branding unless requested.
- No Master Builder demonstration content in user work.
- No inferred details about the uploader.
- No internal prompt language visible in publication prose.

### 8. Release

- Version and state are correct.
- Source lineage is complete.
- Open risks are declared.
- Files and formats are inventoried.
- The final round trip passes or exceptions are approved.

## Report format

For each finding, provide:

- severity: blocking, major, moderate, minor;
- artifact and location;
- issue;
- evidence;
- proposed correction;
- whether canonical approval is required.
