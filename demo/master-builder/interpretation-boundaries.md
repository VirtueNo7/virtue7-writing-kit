# Interpretation Boundaries

## Supported interpretations

The dossier supports exploring:

- the difference between technical skill and trustworthy judgement;
- how growing scale changes the responsibilities of craft;
- reluctance to challenge authority as a professional weakness;
- failure as an event with shared causes and personal responsibility;
- correction through changed systems rather than declarations;
- the relationship between experience, documentation, and verification;
- mastery as the ability to teach, collaborate, and relinquish sole authorship.

## Unsupported or prohibited interpretations

Do not claim that:

- Elias was traumatised, depressed, addicted, abusive, criminal, corrupt, or clinically disordered;
- the North Quay failure killed or injured anyone;
- Elias knowingly used dangerous materials;
- one villain caused the failure;
- the suspension reduced him to poverty;
- Cedar Court or Lantern Hall erased the earlier damage;
- Mara merely inherited Elias's completed wisdom;
- Elias invented building inspection or a universal engineering method;
- the story proves a scientific law about character;
- any uploader, recipient, profession, or real person resembles Elias.

## Narrative fairness

- Present the understandable pressures without using them to excuse the signed approval.
- Present responsibility without turning a whole life into one verdict.
- Show later process changes through evidence.
- Allow Ana, Nora, Mara, Helen, and Tomas independent roles rather than using them as scenery around Elias.
- Preserve the difference between documented act and inferred motive.
