# Demonstration Validation Checklist

## Kernel

- [ ] States that the subject is fictional.
- [ ] Defines a central question or transformation.
- [ ] Includes responsibility, verification, collaboration, and teaching.
- [ ] Avoids reducing the life to failure and redemption.

## Matrix

- [ ] Covers apprenticeship, early intervention, business growth, North Quay, changed practice, restoration, refusal of unsafe uncertainty, teaching, collaboration, and legacy.
- [ ] Gives Ana, Nora, Mara, Helen, and Tomas structurally meaningful roles.
- [ ] Maps each major claim to a synthetic source ID.
- [ ] Identifies interpretation separately from fact.

## Representative unit

- [ ] Explains the project failure clearly to a general reader.
- [ ] Preserves multiple causation.
- [ ] States that no one was injured.
- [ ] Does not invent dialogue or quotations.
- [ ] Shows the immediate professional and material consequences.
- [ ] Connects later process changes without claiming that later work erases the failure.
- [ ] Produces a compressed paragraph, sentence, question, and kernel relation.

## White-label and privacy

- [ ] Contains no Virtue7 branding in the biography output.
- [ ] Contains no recipient-specific assumptions.
- [ ] Contains no recipient-specific profession or biography references.
- [ ] Demonstration details do not leak into unrelated user work.

## Round trip

- [ ] The compressed unit recovers the approved biography kernel.
- [ ] No material boundary disappears.
- [ ] No interpretation becomes fact.
