# People and Relationships

All people are fictional.

## Ana Vale

Elias's spouse and business partner. Ana maintained contracts, cash flow, correspondence, insurance, and restitution records. She repeatedly urged Elias to document verbal changes and challenge clients in writing. She is not merely a supportive spouse in the narrative; the business depended on her judgement and administrative discipline.

## Soren Bale

Elias's apprenticeship master. Bale taught measurement, joinery, sequencing, and the obligation to understand what later trades would rely upon. His assessment identified Elias's precision and his reluctance to disagree openly. Bale died in 1984 and had no involvement in the North Quay project.

## Nora Vale

Elias and Ana's daughter, later a civil engineer. Nora respected her father's craft but challenged his tendency to treat experiential judgement as sufficient documentation. Their professional disagreements became productive after 1990. Do not invent family estrangement.

## Mara Senn

Elias's apprentice from 2010 and later collaborator on Lantern Hall. Mara brought digital modelling and accessibility expertise. Elias did not simply pass down an unchanged tradition; the final project combined his site judgement with methods he had not mastered himself.

## Helen Orm

Public works manager for the North Quay School annex. She transmitted timetable pressure and approved the shortened inspection window. The dossier does not support depicting her as corrupt or indifferent. She worked within a politically fixed opening date and later accepted institutional failings.

## Tomas Wren

Timber supplier whose substituted batch included inadequately graded pieces. The inquiry found failures in both supplier documentation and site verification. Do not make him the sole villain or use invented confrontation scenes.
